Introducing Readability

Written English is becoming more readable, utilizing shorter sentences and words. This concept known as readability finds its roots in the theory that English is growing to become more conversational.

A vareity of formulas have been created to measure this trend. There are a number of variations on this but they essentially measure vocabulary difficulty. Shown here is a simplified version of the Flesch-Kincaid formula that measures Sentence Length and Vocabulary length.

Readable Newspapers

The readability trend has been observed in many places, the mainstream origins of this were first applied on a major scale with newspapers where improvements in readability were shown to increase newspaper circulation.

Shown here are a selection of studies highlighting readability trends in certain sources. The graph is a modified version of the Gunning Fog readability index that translates readability measures to approximate reading levels by grade of the United States schools

Readable Everything

The trend to readable English has been observed in formats outside of Newspapers including, especially in books. The US government has also promoted readable writing and regulated some instances where readable writing must be done.

For reference, popular social media posts are done at a low readability level.

Domain Exception

However, not all English allows for high readability. Of note are domain sources like academic papers, government writing and business related reading. These instances require specific language that can include long words specific to that source.

Model of Comprehension

While there is no universally accepted model of how reading comprehension works, cognitive psychologist Walter Kintsch has gained acclaim for his construction-integration model. In this model comprehension is defined as not just the decoding of words and sentences but also making connections of ideas and deriving meaning from a written text.
In this model, there are three levels that work in parallel to process and meaning out of text.

Linguistic Representation

The surface form of the text representing the exact words and syntatctic structures of the text.

Propostional Abstraction

How words and ideas within the text combine to form a semantic idea, essentially the relationship of words in a text. This forms the textbase, the interconnected representation of the propositions, linked through shared arguments.

Situation Model

The highest level of text comprehension that involves integrating ideas represented in the first two levels with prior knowledge.

Impact of Readability

Readability will have the most impact on the lower levels of processing, making it easier to process words and derive meaning. However the highest level of integration where previous knowledge is derived requires drawing on a previous knowledge.
