Introducing Readability

Written English is becoming more readable, utilizing shorter sentences and words. This concept known as readability finds its roots in the theory that English is growing to become more conversational.

A vareity of formulas have been created to measure this trend. There are a number of variations on this but they essentially measure vocabulary difficulty. Shown here is a simplified version of the Flesch-Kincaid formula that measures Sentence Length and Vocabulary length.

Readable Newspapers

The readability trend has been observed in many places, the mainstream origins of this were first applied on a major scale with newspapers where improvements in readability were shown to increase newspaper circulation.

Shown here are a selection of studies highlighting readability trends in certain sources. The graph is a modified version of the Gunning Fog readability index that translates readability measures to approximate reading levels by grade of the United States schools

Readable Everything

The trend to readable English has been observed in formats outside of Newspapers including, especially in books. The US government has also promoted readable writing and regulated some instances where readable writing must be done.

For reference, popular social media posts are done at a low readability level.

Domain Exception

However, not all English allows for high readability. Of note are domain sources like academic papers, government writing and business related reading. These instances require specific language that can include long words specific to that source.

The Nation's Report Card

The National Assessment of Educational Progress (NAEP) is the largest and nationally representative assessment of what U.S. students know, the results are referred to as the Nation's Report Card.

For the reading comprehension portion, students read grade-appropriate texts and are asked questions that assess their interpretative and critical understanding.

Scores are aggregated into achievement levels for each grade where:

Basic indicates partial mastery of reading comprehension at the current grade.

Proficient represents solid competency of reading comprhension at the current grade and application of knowledge to real world situations.

Advanced presumes mastery of Basic and Proficient

Grade: Needs Improvement

Shown here are the national results for 12th graders which shows a slow decline since 1992, the furthest currently available dataset for 12th graders.

It gets worse

The assessment gets more concerning as reading ability percentiles are considered. Students in the lowest groups continue to fall further and further behind which becomes increasingly harder to correct as these students mature into adults.

The causes of this are mixed. One positive aspect is that high-school graduation rates are higher, where in the past students who struggled with reading would likely drop-out. However, as they stay on students continue to struggle with reading and lack of funding in certain school distrcits means teachers cannot dedicate the appropriate time to these students in need.

 

State-level Variances

Scores also vary at the state-level which reflects America's lack of standardization when it comes to determing what gets taught. Currently students are taught consistent reading fundamentals but additional subject learning differs by state.

Some distrcits emphasize additional reading strategies focused on test taking. However cognitive scientists believe more emphasis should be put on background knowledge and learning keywords.

Reading Comprehension

Reading comprehension is the process of reading text to understand its meaning to integrate with what the reader already knows.

Construction-Integration

In the construction-integration model, the comprehension process is shown in blue to represent the three levels that work in parallel to process and meaning out of text. The white areas represent the start from text to understanding by the reader.

Readability Impacts Construction

Readability will have the most impact on the lower levels of processing, where the actual text meaning is constrcuted.

However in the situation model, where the text meaning is integrated with prior knowledge, benefits can only be derived with a greater knowledge base.

Longform Text

Additional challenges are presented in the construction level when text increases in length as it creates additional relationships and words to define.
